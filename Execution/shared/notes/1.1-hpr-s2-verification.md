# Cross-Agent Note: HPr S2 Citation — UNVERIFIED, Escalation Required

## Finding (Critical for T5)

The manifest citation "van Nuland et al., 1995, JMB 246:180" for HPr 15N
relaxation S2 data is **WRONG**. Direct PubMed verification (PMID 7853396)
confirms the paper is titled "High-resolution structure of the phosphorylated
form of the histidine-containing phosphocarrier protein HPr from Escherichia
coli determined by restrained molecular dynamics from NMR-NOE data." It is a
**structure-determination paper based on NOE distance restraints**, not a
dynamics paper. It contains NO T1/T2/NOE relaxation data and NO S2 order
parameters.

## Alternative Sources Searched

A literature and BMRB search found:

- No BMRB entry for E. coli or B. subtilis HPr that contains deposited
  T1/T2/NOE or S2 values. HPr BMRB entries (50934, 18379, 17274, 17095,
  4264, 2371 for E. coli; 4972, 932 for B. subtilis) contain chemical shifts
  and (for 17095) kinetic rates, but not relaxation parameters suitable for
  Lipari-Szabo analysis.
- PubMed searches for "HPr" + "15N relaxation" + "S2" / "order parameter" /
  "Lipari-Szabo" did not surface a clear match.
- Related proteins (glutaredoxin, thioredoxin, adenylate kinase, glucose
  permease IIA) have the same-era 15N relaxation studies, but HPr itself
  does not appear to have a similar published study at 500-600 MHz with
  Lipari-Szabo analysis accessible via the searches performed.
- The Implementation Plan had rated HPr S2 quality as "Moderate" with ~65%
  coverage — this ranking may have originated from a citation to a paper
  that does not actually contain the S2 data claimed.

## T5 Impact

**This puts T5 at risk.** If HPr's S2 reference cannot be located, HPr is
unusable for the Alpha-M quantitative S2 comparison, and T5 drops from 12
proteins to 11.

**Mitigating factor**: The concurrently-running protein-addition agent is
adding 4 new proteins (NTL9, ACBP, FKBP12, EnHD) to the manifest. Assuming
all 4 are BMRB-confirmed for S2 data, post-addition T5 count becomes:
- With HPr: 12 + 4 = 16 (well above threshold)
- Without HPr: 11 + 4 = 15 (well above threshold, 3-protein margin)

HPr's loss would NOT threaten T5 once the 4 additions are complete. But
HPr itself would then be a stability/structural-comparison-only protein,
not a quantitative S2 target — similar to Crambin.

## Escalation

Writing `shared/help-needed/head-1.1-hpr-s2.md` to request PlannerAI or
human decision on:

1. **Option A**: Accept HPr as S2-quality-unverified; retain in benchmark as
   "classical MD + generative comparison, but no quantitative S2 comparison."
   Document as a known limitation.
2. **Option B**: Deeper literature search (institutional database access,
   Google Scholar time-filter) to locate a definitive HPr S2 paper. Human
   or research librarian assistance may be needed.
3. **Option C**: Drop HPr from the Alpha-M benchmark entirely. T5 still holds
   at 15 (12 original - HPr + 4 new). This is the cleanest option if no S2
   source can be verified.

## Recommendation

**Option A** is the minimal-disruption path: retain HPr but flag S2 as
unverified; keep it as a "structural/classical comparison" protein only.
This preserves the 85 aa size-class representation in the benchmark
(between CspA at 70 aa and Barnase at 110 aa) without making unfounded
quantitative S2 claims.
