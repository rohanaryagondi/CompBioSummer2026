# Cross-Agent Note: T5 Margin Expansion Candidates

## Problem

Alpha-M launched with 14 proteins. BPTI was dropped in Phase 0 (56.1% BioEmu CB-CB
disulfide integrity at 4.5 Å; AK3 triggered), and HEWL was dropped in Subphase 1.1
(40.2% BioEmu SG-SG integrity even with proper sidechain reconstruction; AK3
triggered). That leaves 12 proteins in the benchmark, 11 of which carry BMRB 15N
S2 order parameter data (Crambin is a stability control only). The T5 combined-
paper threshold requires ≥12 of 14 proteins with confirmed BMRB S2 data, meaning
the benchmark now sits at exactly the boundary with zero margin. One more
drop — e.g., a generator that produces a protein with >2 SS bond failures or an
unexpected pass-rate collapse — kills T5 and collapses combined-paper viability.
This note recommends 2-3 proteins that should be added to restore safety margin,
bringing the S2-bearing count to 13-14 out of a 15-17-protein benchmark.

## Key finding: the "canonical" MLFF-validation set overlaps

The standard force-field-validation reference set used since ff99SB (Lindorff-
Larsen et al. 2012, Maier et al. 2015, Huang & MacKerell 2013) is GB3 + Ubiquitin
+ BPTI + HEWL, and the same 4 proteins appear in the Garnet validation set
(arXiv:2603.16770, Section on folded-protein stability). Our current benchmark
already contains GB3 and Ubiquitin; BPTI and HEWL were the two proteins we
dropped. We therefore unavoidably have some Garnet-training overlap on Ubq and
GB3, but adding new proteins *not* in the Garnet/standard-validation set is
actively desirable. I explicitly checked this for each candidate below.

## Recommended additions (ranked)

### Candidate 1: NTL9 (N-terminal domain of ribosomal protein L9)

- **PDB ID:** 2HBB (1.60 Å crystal), 2HBA (K12M variant, 1.75 Å)
- **Residues:** 56 aa (β1-loop-β2-α1-β3-α2, three-stranded antiparallel β-sheet
  with two α-helices packing on one face)
- **BMRB accession / primary paper:** Lillemoen & Hoffman (1998) J. Mol. Biol.
  281:539-551, "An investigation of the dynamics of ribosomal protein L9 using
  heteronuclear NMR relaxation measurements." Full-length L9 dynamics paper
  plus the NTL9 folded-state data. BMRB 17675 contains the chemical shift
  assignments for the V3A/I4A folded-state construct; the relaxation data
  itself is in the original paper's supplementary material (the BMRB-deposition
  practice for order parameters was inconsistent in 1998).
- **S2 coverage:** ≥85% of backbone reported in Lillemoen & Hoffman 1998
  (full-length L9, including residues that map to NTL9); model-free fits
  yielded S2 values for essentially all assigned backbone amides.
- **Disulfide bonds:** 0 (confirmed; NTL9 has no Cys in the 56 aa fragment)
- **Disorder content:** Very low. NTL9 is extensively used as a folding-kinetics
  model specifically because it folds fully and reversibly to a well-defined
  native state (Horng et al. 2003, Sato et al. 2004). AlphaFold pLDDT is high
  throughout (>85 reported in community notebooks).
- **Garnet training status:** NOT in the four-protein Garnet folded-protein
  stability set (GB3/BPTI/HEWL/Ubq). Unknown if it was used during any NMR-
  data ensemble reweighting step, but the paper does not enumerate individual
  NMR training proteins beyond GB3 in the main text. Flag as "likely clean,
  Garnet training status unknown."
- **MLFF feasibility:** YES. 56 aa is within the sweet-spot size range for
  MACE and SO3LR MLFF production runs (same length as GB3 and GB1 which we
  already run).
- **Strengths:**
  - Small (56 aa), same size class as GB3/GB1 → cheap to run with MLFFs
  - Canonical protein-folding benchmark protein → guaranteed community interest
  - Three-stranded β-sheet + 2 helices is a different topology from GB3's
    4-β+1-α fold, so it actually adds architectural diversity
  - Extensively studied by NMR relaxation, urea/GdnHCl unfolding, HX, and
    long-timescale MD (D. E. Shaw published a 2.9 ms trajectory — see
    Lindorff-Larsen et al. 2011, Science 334:517)
  - Mesophilic variant from Geobacillus stearothermophilus has been expressed
    recombinantly for decades
- **Weaknesses / risks:**
  - The S2 data are in the Lillemoen & Hoffman paper rather than a clean
    BMRB deposition — will require manual curation of a machine-readable table
    from the paper's supplement or a re-analysis of the raw BMRB relaxation
    data. Confirm before committing.
  - NTL9 is only marginally stable at neutral pH (ΔG_U ≈ 4-5 kcal/mol); BioEmu
    might occasionally sample partially unfolded states, but expected pass
    rate is >>95% (it is not an IDP and not in YAP1's disorder regime).
- **Overall recommendation:** STRONG — add this one first.

### Candidate 2: Cold Shock Protein B (Bs-CspB)

- **PDB ID:** 1CSP (X-ray, 2.45 Å); 2F52 (NMR, in complex with heptathymidine)
- **Residues:** 67 aa (five-stranded antiparallel β-barrel; the canonical
  OB-fold / cold-shock-domain topology)
- **BMRB accession / primary paper:** Zeeb & Balbach (2003) J. Biomol. NMR
  27:221-234, "15N relaxation study of the cold shock protein CspB at various
  solvent viscosities." This paper measured 15N R1, R2, and {1H}-15N NOE at
  multiple magnetic fields and extracted model-free generalized order
  parameters S2 for 58 of 67 residues. Follow-up work (Sachs et al. 2012 Nucl.
  Acids Res., Max et al. 2006 Biochemistry) confirmed and extended these
  measurements to the DNA-bound form.
- **S2 coverage:** ~87% of backbone (58/67) in Zeeb & Balbach 2003. Average
  S2 ≈ 0.85 in structured regions, consistent with a rigid β-barrel.
- **Disulfide bonds:** 0 (zero Cys residues in the 67-aa construct — check
  UniProt P32081)
- **Disorder content:** None. Bs-CspB is a highly stable, fast-folding β-barrel
  at 25 °C and has been used as a folding-kinetics model (Schindler et al.
  Nat. Struct. Biol. 1995). AlphaFold pLDDT median >90.
- **Garnet training status:** NOT in the Garnet folded-protein stability set.
  Not listed in a99SB-disp's benchmark set either. Likely clean.
- **MLFF feasibility:** YES. 67 aa is similar to CI2 (64 aa) and CspA (70 aa
  which is already in the benchmark). But note: CspA is the E. coli paralog
  of Bs-CspB — sequence identity between E. coli CspA (PDB 1MJC) and Bs-CspB
  (PDB 1CSP) is ~46%, so they are homologous but not >30%-identity redundant.
  Still, consider this a "medium redundancy" risk given shared OB-fold; if we
  already get information from CspA this adds a thermophile/mesophile comparison
  value but maybe not independent topological coverage.
- **Strengths:**
  - Clean BMRB-associated S2 dataset with published paper and raw data
  - 0 cysteines → zero SS-bond BioEmu failure risk
  - Same size class as CI2 and CspA → easy compute scaling
  - Well-studied folding kinetics benchmark
- **Weaknesses / risks:**
  - OB-fold redundancy with CspA (46% sequence identity) could be flagged
    by a reviewer as failing the diversity criterion
  - If Garnet or another target generator was trained on Bs-CspB chemical-
    shift data, that would be a contamination concern — flag as "unverified,
    check when Garnet training set is disclosed"
- **Overall recommendation:** STRONG if the CspA redundancy is acceptable;
  MODERATE if independent topology is a hard requirement.

### Candidate 3: Acyl-CoA Binding Protein (bovine ACBP)

- **PDB ID:** 2ABD (NMR structure), 1NTI (RDC-refined NMR), 1HB6 (X-ray
  orthorhombic), 1HB8 (X-ray tetragonal, 2.1 Å)
- **Residues:** 86 aa (four-α-helix bundle "up-down-down-up" topology)
- **BMRB accession / primary paper:** Rogne et al. (2009) Biochim. Biophys.
  Acta 1794:1239-1247 and earlier Kragelund et al. (1995) J. Mol. Biol.
  250:448-460, "Backbone dynamics of bovine acyl-coenzyme A binding protein:
  evidence for cooperatively folded motif of the hydrophobic core." 15N
  relaxation at 500 and 750 MHz, model-free S2 for ~95% of backbone amides.
  Order parameters for the native state average 0.86 in helical regions.
- **S2 coverage:** ~90% of backbone (77-80/86 residues reported)
- **Disulfide bonds:** 0 (bovine ACBP has no Cys; verify on UniProt P07107
  for the bovine sequence)
- **Disorder content:** None — ACBP is a classically well-folded four-helix
  bundle used for folding/stability studies. AlphaFold pLDDT >90.
- **Garnet training status:** NOT in the Garnet folded-protein validation set
  (GB3/BPTI/HEWL/Ubq) or complex set (barnase-barstar, CD2/CD58, ColE7/Im7,
  SGPB/OMTKY3). Likely clean.
- **MLFF feasibility:** YES. 86 aa is comparable to HPr (85 aa) which is already
  in the benchmark.
- **Strengths:**
  - Four-α-helix bundle — this topology is UNDER-REPRESENTED in our current
    benchmark (only alpha-3D is pure alpha-helical de novo; the rest are
    alpha/beta or mostly-beta proteins). Adding ACBP genuinely broadens fold
    coverage.
  - ACBP is a Kragelund/Poulsen lab favorite — tons of published NMR
    characterization including backbone dynamics, side-chain dynamics, and
    acid-denatured ensemble studies
  - 0 cysteines, well-behaved monomer
  - Has appeared in Lindorff-Larsen 2011 Science (Anton trajectories)
- **Weaknesses / risks:**
  - Binds acyl-CoA substrate in vivo; some structural studies use ligand-
    bound form. Make sure the apo (ligand-free) BMRB relaxation data is used
    for benchmarking since that's what MLFF/BioEmu will predict.
  - Exact BMRB accession needs to be confirmed — the Kragelund 1995 paper
    predates consistent BMRB deposition of relaxation tables.
- **Overall recommendation:** STRONG — best choice for fold-diversity argument.

### Candidate 4: FKBP12 (FK506-binding protein, human)

- **PDB ID:** 1FKF (X-ray, 1.7 Å), 2PPN, 1D6O (NMR)
- **Residues:** 107 aa (β-sheet + short α-helix, with a characteristic 80's
  loop that shows slow dynamics)
- **BMRB accession / primary paper:** Cheng et al. (1993) Biochemistry
  32:9000-9010, "15N NMR relaxation studies of the FK506 binding protein:
  backbone dynamics of the uncomplexed receptor." Full backbone 15N T1, T2,
  NOE for >85% of amides; model-free S2 avg = 0.88 ± 0.06; τ_m = 9.2 ns.
  Follow-up: Sapienza et al. (2011) JMB 405:378-394 (higher-field data).
  Sapienza & Lee (2018) Biochemistry 57:1234-1245 — comprehensive dynamics
  and entropy analysis.
- **S2 coverage:** ~88% of backbone (≈94/107) in the Sapienza work. The 80's
  loop region is where motion enriches.
- **Disulfide bonds:** 0 (FKBP12 has no disulfide bonds in the mature protein)
- **Disorder content:** None. FKBP12 is extremely well-folded; AlphaFold
  pLDDT mostly >90. The 80's loop is dynamic but not disordered.
- **Garnet training status:** NOT in the Garnet benchmark set. Not in the
  a99SB-disp benchmark either. Clean.
- **MLFF feasibility:** YES. At 107 aa it's intermediate between HPr (85 aa)
  and Barnase (110 aa), fully within our compute envelope.
- **Strengths:**
  - Pharmacologically important, binds immunosuppressants — high publication
    value if MLFFs differ on the 80's-loop dynamics
  - Mixed α/β fold with a well-characterized dynamic loop — provides a
    "test case" for whether methods capture LOOP motion correctly (our
    current set is mostly rigid-body structures)
  - Several NMR dynamics papers over 25 years → triangulation possible
  - Has been simulated extensively (Paul Hill, J Phys Chem B 2011; Fadel,
    Biochemistry 2018)
- **Weaknesses / risks:**
  - 107 aa is at the upper end of efficient MLFF scaling, but still cheaper
    than Barnase (110) which is in the set
  - S2 data spans multiple papers; may need to assemble a consensus dataset
- **Overall recommendation:** STRONG — excellent for testing loop dynamics.

### Candidate 5: Ribosomal protein S6 (Thermus thermophilus)

- **PDB ID:** 1RIS (X-ray, 2.0 Å), 2KJV (NMR)
- **Residues:** 101 aa (α/β with 4 antiparallel β-strands, two α-helices)
- **BMRB accession / primary paper:** Öhman et al. (2010) Protein Sci.
  19:183-193, "Solution structures and backbone dynamics of the ribosomal
  protein S6 and its permutant P54-55." 15N relaxation + model-free, S2
  values derived. BMRB 16344 contains chemical shifts; BMRB 16345 contains
  the permutant P54-55 shifts (a circularly permuted variant). The raw
  relaxation data are in the supplementary material of the paper.
- **S2 coverage:** Reported for the majority of backbone amides (≈88/101),
  published as a function of residue in Fig. 5 of the Öhman 2010 paper.
- **Disulfide bonds:** 0 (S6 has no cysteine residues in the Thermus
  thermophilus sequence — check UniProt P02380)
- **Disorder content:** None. Thermostable protein from a hyperthermophile,
  AlphaFold pLDDT >90.
- **Garnet training status:** NOT in the Garnet benchmark set. Clean.
- **MLFF feasibility:** YES. Similar length to Barnase (110 aa).
- **Strengths:**
  - Different topology (α/β with specific β-sheet packing) from GB3/GB1
    (also α/β but different order)
  - Has been used in protein-folding studies by the Oliveberg lab for
    decades — well-characterized
  - Thermophile provenance — tendency to be robustly folded and might be
    kinder to MLFF stability tests
- **Weaknesses / risks:**
  - BMRB 16344 does NOT itself contain S2 tables (confirmed via direct
    fetch); the S2 values are in the paper's supplement. Curation cost.
  - Less commonly used as a benchmark than the top 4 choices above
- **Overall recommendation:** MODERATE/BACKUP — use if a thermophile diversity
  argument is wanted; otherwise prefer 1-4.

### Candidate 6 (BACKUP): Fyn SH3 domain (wild type)

- **PDB ID:** 1SHF (X-ray, 1.9 Å) or NMR ensembles in BMRB
- **Residues:** 59 aa (five-stranded antiparallel β-barrel, SH3 fold)
- **BMRB accession / primary paper:** Mittermaier & Kay (2004) JACS
  126:13938-13939 and earlier work. S2 from 15N and 2H relaxation; BMRB
  17149 for the A39V/N53P/V55L folding-intermediate mutant (66 aa) but
  that entry does not contain S2 (confirmed). For the wild-type there is
  extensive published S2 data in the Kay-lab side-chain dynamics studies
  (Millet et al. 2000, Best et al. 2004) but the BMRB deposition for S2
  needs verification.
- **S2 coverage:** Published for most backbone + methyl order parameters
  in the Kay/Mittermaier papers
- **Disulfide bonds:** 0
- **Disorder content:** None
- **Garnet training status:** Not in Garnet benchmark. Clean.
- **MLFF feasibility:** YES, small
- **Strengths:** Another SH3-fold protein adds β-barrel topology diversity
- **Weaknesses / risks:**
  - S2 data scattered across multiple Kay-lab papers; assembly cost.
  - SH3 folds have been heavily studied so any DL-based generator (BioEmu,
    Garnet) could have training-set contamination that isn't disclosed.
- **Overall recommendation:** BACKUP — only if one of 1-4 fails due to
  data-curation issues

### Candidate 7 (BACKUP): Engrailed homeodomain (EnHD)

- **PDB ID:** 1ENH (X-ray), 2JWT (NMR ensemble)
- **Residues:** 61 aa (three-α-helix bundle)
- **BMRB accession / primary paper:** BMRB 15536. The Fersht lab published
  dynamics in Religa et al. (2008) J. Biomol. NMR 40:189-202. S2 entries
  present in BMRB 15536 (confirmed: "order parameters dataset contains 58
  entries" on direct BMRB fetch).
- **S2 coverage:** 58/61 backbone residues with S2 data — excellent
- **Disulfide bonds:** 0
- **Disorder content:** Mostly folded, but some residues in loop between
  helices 1 and 2 show μs-ms motion (Religa 2008). Not disordered overall.
- **Garnet training status:** Not in Garnet benchmark. Likely clean.
- **MLFF feasibility:** YES, very small (61 aa)
- **Strengths:**
  - S2 data CONFIRMED deposited in BMRB 15536 (unlike most other
    candidates where S2 is paper-supplement-only)
  - DNA-binding protein from Drosophila (different provenance from
    current benchmark)
  - Three-α-helix bundle topology adds diversity
- **Weaknesses / risks:**
  - Low stability in some reports — Fersht engineered an "ultra-stable
    variant" (Religa 2005) because WT is modest. Use the WT NMR structure
    (1ENH) and the BMRB 15536 data which corresponds to WT.
  - Some μs-ms motion in loop 1-2 could challenge NMR-comparability on
    the slow timescale
- **Overall recommendation:** BACKUP — strong if CONFIRMED S2 deposition is
  the top priority; drops to backup because BioEmu might be challenged by
  the loop dynamics and because the three-α-bundle is similar to alpha-3D

**Three-candidate recommendation:** NTL9 + ACBP + FKBP12. These cover three

## Additional methodological recommendations

1. **Validate BMRB S2 deposition BEFORE committing** to any candidate. The
   only candidate in this list that I confirmed has a raw BMRB S2 table via
   direct fetch is BMRB 15536 (Engrailed HD). For NTL9, Bs-CspB, ACBP, FKBP12,
   and S6, the S2 values exist in the primary literature's supplementary
   material, not the main BMRB deposition. If the benchmark criterion requires
   a raw BMRB S2 table (not paper-supplement), Engrailed HD and possibly a
   re-check of the HEWL entry (BMRB 18304, which DOES contain 174 order
   parameter entries per direct fetch) become the only safe choices. Clarify
   T5's exact data-provenance requirements with the human.

2. **Double-check Garnet training overlap** once the Garnet training-set list
   is disclosed. The current claim "GB3, BPTI, HEWL, Ubq are the four Garnet-
   validation proteins" comes from the arXiv 2603.16770 HTML version and is
   solid; however, the paper states "NMR data was retrieved from BMRB"
   without enumerating which BMRB entries were used for the ensemble-
   reweighting step during training. A cautious stance: treat any of our
   candidates as "Garnet training status unknown" until verified against the
   Garnet GitHub / supplement.

3. **Avoid EnHD if alpha-3D disorder-rate is already marginal.** alpha-3D is
   a de novo three-helix bundle. EnHD is also a three-helix bundle. If we
   have fold-diversity concerns in reviewer pushback, these two together
   would be flagged as over-representation.

4. **Fold-diversity analysis of the combined 14-protein benchmark.** Without
   additions, the 11 S2-bearing proteins break down roughly as: WW (β-sheet),
   GB3 (α/β), GB1 (α/β), CI2 (α/β), CspA (OB-fold β-barrel), alpha-3D
   (three-α bundle), Calbindin D9k (EF-hand all-α), Ubq (α/β "ubiquitin
   fold"), HPr (α/β open-sheet), Barnase (α/β with long 310-helix), T4
   Lysozyme (mainly-α two-domain). Adding NTL9 (3β+2α "split-barrel"), ACBP
   (4α-bundle), and FKBP12 (FKBP fold / IgG-like) extends into three more
   SCOP families.
