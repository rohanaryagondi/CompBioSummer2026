# Cross-Agent Note: T4 Lysozyme S2 Citation — Reference State Correction

## Finding

The verification audit (2026-04-17) flagged the manifest citation for T4L S2:
"Mulder et al., 2001, Biochemistry 40:4458" as needing direct verification of
WT vs. cavity-mutant. **Verification result: the paper is the L99A cavity
mutant, NOT WT T4L.**

Specifically: Biochemistry 40:4458 is "Flexibility and Ligand Exchange in a
Buried Cavity Mutant of T4 Lysozyme Studied by Multinuclear NMR" by Mulder,
Skrynnikov, Hon, Dahlquist, Kay (2001). The "buried cavity mutant" is the
L99A variant, which has a large hydrophobic cavity in the C-terminal domain
and exhibits fast conformational exchange (~1500 s⁻¹) in the cavity region.
This is a **reference-state mismatch with PDB 3LZM (WT T4L)**.

Additional 2001 Mulder/Kay T4L papers all study cavity mutants (L99A or
double mutants), not WT:
- JACS 123:967 (2001): Asn/Gln side-chain dynamics in L99A cavity mutant
- Nat Struct Biol 8:932 (2001): Excited-state dynamics in L99A
- Other 2001 papers: all L99A or related cavity mutants

## Implication

Running MD on WT T4L (3LZM) and comparing to L99A S2 introduces a systematic
reference-state error primarily localized to the cavity region (residues
~86-115, helix C, and surrounding β-sheet). The L99A mutation locally
destabilizes and increases sub-ns motion at cavity-lining residues; outside
the cavity, S2 values are comparable to WT per published side-by-side
comparisons in the Kay lab.

## Candidate WT T4L S2 References

A literature search did not immediately identify a dedicated 1990s WT-T4L
15N-relaxation / Lipari-Szabo S2 paper equivalent to Mulder 2001. Candidates
found:

1. **McIntosh, Wand, Lowry, Redfield, Dahlquist 1990 Biochemistry** —
   "Assignment of the backbone 1H and 15N NMR resonances of bacteriophage
   T4 lysozyme" (PMID 2207079). Assignments only; likely not T1/T2/NOE or
   S2 directly.
2. **McIntosh & Dahlquist 1987 PNAS 84:1244** — "Proton NMR measurements of
   bacteriophage T4 lysozyme aided by 15N isotopic labeling". Structural
   /dynamic, but mostly ¹H focus, not Lipari-Szabo S2.
3. **Goto, Skrynnikov, Dahlquist, Kay 2001 JMB 308:745** — More T4L dynamics
   but on L99A (same cavity mutant track).
4. **Meiler, Prompers, Peti, Griesinger, Brüschweiler 2001 JACS** — "Model-
   free approach to the dynamic interpretation of residual dipolar couplings
   in globular proteins" using WT T4L as one of the test proteins (T4L S2
   values reported).

The audit suggestion to search McIntosh/Dahlquist 1990s did not find a
specific Lipari-Szabo WT T4L paper in the PubMed/web results. **A deeper
literature search is needed in Phase 1 or early Phase 2.**
