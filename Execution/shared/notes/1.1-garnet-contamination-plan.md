# Cross-Agent Note: Garnet Contamination Stratification Plan for Phase 2

## Background

Garnet (Blanco-González et al. 2026 arXiv:2603.16770) is an AI-derived protein
force field that we will benchmark against classical MD + generative ensembles
in Alpha-M. Garnet was trained on GB3 and validated on a specific set of
proteins including BPTI, HEWL, Ubiquitin, barnase-barstar complex, and several
others. **Several proteins in our Alpha-M benchmark overlap with Garnet's
training and validation sets, creating a data-leakage risk.**

To report Garnet's Alpha-M S2 accuracy honestly, we must stratify the 12-16
protein benchmark by contamination status and report performance separately
for each stratum. This note documents the stratification plan and the
statistical test that Phase 2 MUST perform.

## Analysis Plan for Phase 2 Garnet Benchmarking

The Phase 2 Alpha-M subphase plan MUST include the following specific tasks
when analyzing Garnet's S2 predictions.

### Task G1: Stratified S2 Accuracy Report

Compute, for each stratum separately:

- **R² (predicted vs experimental S2)** per-protein and pooled within stratum
- **Mean absolute error (MAE)** in S2 units
- **Kendall τ** rank correlation per-protein
- Stratum-pooled 95% confidence intervals via bootstrap (10,000 resamples)

Present as a table:

| Stratum | N | R² (pooled) | MAE | Median τ |
|---------|---|-------------|-----|----------|
| Training | 1 | GB3 only | — | — |
| Validation | 2 | Ubq, Barn | — | — |
| Clean (held-out) | 13 | all others | — | — |

### Task G2: Leakage Signal Test

Test the null hypothesis that Garnet's held-out-clean R² is NOT worse than
its training R². Statistical test:

- **Bootstrap comparison**: Compute R²(training stratum) - R²(clean stratum)
  with 10,000 bootstrap resamples of the underlying residue-level S2 pairs.
- **Decision rule**: If R²(training) - R²(clean) > 0.10 with 95% CI excluding
  zero, treat as "expected leakage signal" — Garnet performs better on
  contaminated proteins than held-out.
- **Report either way**: Even if no significant drop, report the magnitude
  with CI. Honest reporting is required for the combined paper.

### Task G3: Methods-Section Language

The combined paper Methods section must include the stratification
language explicitly. Draft:

> "Garnet's S2 accuracy was reported separately for three strata reflecting
> its training and validation history. The training stratum (GB3) was used
> by Blanco-González et al. to fit Garnet force-field parameters; the
> validation stratum (ubiquitin, barnase) was used during model development
> for hyperparameter tuning and reported performance. The clean stratum
> (all other benchmark proteins, N=13) has not been seen by Garnet in any
> form. We report R² and MAE for each stratum and test the held-out
> performance against the contaminated subset via bootstrap. A significant
> drop in R² (>0.10) on the clean stratum relative to training/validation
> would constitute expected leakage signal and be acknowledged as a
> quantitative measure of Garnet's in-distribution vs out-of-distribution
> performance gap."

### Task G4: Handling of Size / Fold Stratification

Within the clean stratum, additionally stratify by:

- **Fold family**: β-sheet only (WW, CspA), β-barrel/mixed (GB1), all-α
  (α3D, EnHD, T4L), mixed α/β (CI2, Calbindin, HPr, NTL9, ACBP, FKBP12).
  Report per-fold-family R² if any family has N ≥ 3.
- **Size**: <60 aa (WW, Crambin), 60-80 aa (GB1, CI2, CspA, α3D, Calbindin,
  HPr), 80-130 aa (Barnase if moved, NTL9, ACBP, FKBP12, EnHD), >130 aa (T4L).

## Rationale for Honest Reporting

The combined paper (Nature Comp Sci target) will be scrutinized on Garnet
reporting. Reviewers will specifically check whether we've been transparent
about GB3 training contamination. Burying the contamination signal would be
a reviewer-lethal flaw. Presenting stratified results up-front turns what
could be a liability into evidence of analytical rigor: we SHOW the leakage
signal rather than hiding it.
