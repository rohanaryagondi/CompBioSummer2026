# Cross-Agent Note: Calbindin D9k Apo State Lock

## Decision

**APO STATE LOCKED.** Phase 2 MD, MLFF, and generative runs for Calbindin D9k
MUST use the apo (calcium-free) state. The S2 comparison reference is
**Akke et al., 1993, Biochemistry 32:9832-9844** (apo-calbindin D9k). Kordel et al.
1992 Biochemistry 31:4856 (holo-calbindin) MUST NOT be used as the S2 reference
because apo and holo calbindin have materially different backbone dynamics at
the EF-hand loops (fold ratios of 2-5x in loop-region S2).

## Rationale

The manifest entry for calb (Calbindin D9k) declared `state: apo` but the PDB
entry 3ICB is the **Ca²⁺-bound holo form** containing two calcium ions and
sulfate. Running MD on unstripped 3ICB would produce holo-dynamics, which
would be systematically over-ordered at the EF-hand loops relative to the
declared apo S2 reference — a hidden state mismatch that would confound
Alpha-M's comparison of MD vs experimental S2.

The verification audit (2026-04-17) flagged this as YELLOW-level. Locking to
apo matches the manifest declaration, matches the apo S2 reference (Akke 1993),
and is achievable by stripping Ca²⁺ + sulfate from 3ICB at the PDB-cleaning
step of Phase 2 setup.

## Required Preparation Protocol (Phase 2)

The Phase 2 Alpha-M preparation subphase MUST apply the following steps before
building MD systems for Calbindin D9k:

1. **Load 3ICB**. Chain A, residues 1-75 (manifest `residue_range`).
2. **Strip calcium ions**. Remove HETATM records with residue name `CA` that
   are not protein alpha carbons (look for `HETATM ... CA  CA` where the residue
   name is `CA` and NOT a protein residue). Expect 2 Ca²⁺ ions in 3ICB.
3. **Strip sulfate**. Remove HETATM records with residue name `SO4`.
4. **Strip waters** (standard practice; solvent is added fresh during MD setup).
5. **Check for other heteroatoms** and strip any non-protein ligands.
6. **Re-protonate** with pdb2gmx / pdbfixer to re-add hydrogens appropriate for
   the apo form (note: some sidechains near the EF-hand loops may shift pKa in
   the absence of bound Ca²⁺; use pH 6.0-7.0 as the Akke 1993 measurement pH).
7. **Verify stripping**: grep the output PDB for `CA  CA` and `SO4` and confirm
   zero matches before MD energy minimization.

## Why Not Use Kordel 1992 (Holo)

Kordel et al. 1992 Biochemistry 31:4856-4866 measured S2 on the Ca²⁺-loaded form.
In the holo state, the EF-hand loops (residues 20-30 and 56-66 approximately)
are rigidified by Ca²⁺ coordination, producing high S2 values (~0.85-0.90).
In the apo state (Akke 1993), these same loops have S2 values ~0.60-0.75 due
to increased conformational heterogeneity upon Ca²⁺ release. Using Kordel 1992
as the reference while running apo MD would produce a ~0.15-0.25 unit false
deviation in loop S2 that is purely due to the state mismatch, not the MD method.
