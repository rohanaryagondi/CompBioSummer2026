# MACE-OFF24 Implicit-Solvent Pilot — Option 4 Feasibility

## TL;DR

- **Implicit solvent tested:** GBn2 (`amber14-all.xml` + `implicit/gbn2.xml`)
  via `MLPotential.createMixedSystem` on protein-only system (no explicit water).
- **WW ns/day (implicit):** 0.197 measured at step 2000, vs explicit-hybrid
  0.184. Ratio = **1.07× speedup** (not the 10× hoped for).
- **GB3 ns/day (implicit):** see §3 — result appended after job completes.
- **Critical finding: GBn2 + OpenCL is numerically unstable on Langevin
  1 fs.** WW equilibration exploded to T = 40,365 K between step 1000
  (T = 379 K, PE = -242,556 kJ/mol, stable) and step 2000 (T = 40,365 K,
  KE = 268,322 kJ/mol, system destroyed). No NaN; dynamics is numerically
  valid but physically destroyed. Script's NaN-based safety check does not
  fire on such large-but-finite excursions.
- **Phase 2 feasibility at implicit throughput: INFEASIBLE**
- **Recommendation: REJECT Option 4.** Combined with the Langevin instability,
  implicit-solvent MACE on OpenCL is not a path to Phase 2 MACE scope recovery.

## 1. Simulation protocol

- PDB extraction, PDBFixer hydrogens (pH 7), same as
  `mace_hybrid_nvt.py` (Sub 1.1 reference)
- **ForceField:** `amber14-all.xml` + `implicit/gbn2.xml`
- **System build:** `ForceField.createSystem(nonbondedMethod=NoCutoff,
  constraints=None, rigidWater=False)`. No explicit water, no ions, no
  periodic box. Classical system has 6 forces including `CustomGBForce`
  (the GBn2 implicit-solvent body).
- **Hybrid build:** `MLPotential('mace-off24-medium').createMixedSystem(...,
  protein_atoms)` adds `PythonForce` for MACE (all atoms are protein in an
  implicit-solvent system, so `protein_atoms = list(range(N))`). Final
  hybrid: 7 forces. Build time = 3.2 s on WW (534 atoms). API works.
- **Integrator:** LangevinMiddle, 300 K, 1 /ps, dt = 1 fs (matching
  explicit-hybrid protocol for comparability).
- **Platform:** OpenCL (CUDA disabled per Sub 1.1 policy, same as explicit
  hybrid benchmark).
- **Scripts:** `phases/phase-1/subphase-1.1/output/scripts/mace_implicit_nvt.py`
  (driver), `mace_implicit_ww.sbatch`, `mace_implicit_gb3.sbatch`
  (SLURM wrappers, cryptic 8-char job names `x2n7fp5k`, `q8w4vh3t`).

## 2. WW implicit-solvent result (SLURM 8760482)

- **Protein:** Pin1 WW domain (residues 6-39 of 2F21 after PDBFixer; 33 res,
  534 atoms, all treated by MACE).
- **Node:** a1128u08n01 (RTX 5000 Ada, gpu_devel partition).

### Build + minimization

| Stage | Wall time | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| Raw → protein-only → PDBFixer | 1 s | 534 atoms after fixer |
| Classical implicit system | 1 s | 6 forces (incl. CustomGBForce) |
| Hybrid MACE + classical | 3.2 s | 7 forces, OK |
| Energy minimization (2000 iter, tol 10) | **547 s (9.1 min)** | PE = -243,083 kJ/mol |
| Post-min max |force| | 28.7 kJ/mol/nm | cleanly relaxed (well below 1e5 threshold) |

**Compared to explicit hybrid WW minimization (26.9 min in job 8736919):
implicit was 2.95× faster for minimization.** The L-BFGS iterations in
hybrid mode are gated by MACE force evaluations, so the speedup reflects
the reduced per-call cost of not propagating classical-water forces
through L-BFGS.

### Equilibration throughput and stability

| Step | Time (ps) | PE (kJ/mol) | KE (kJ/mol) | Total E | T (K) | ns/day |
|------|-----------|-------------|-------------|---------|-------|--------|
| 1000 | 1.000 | -242,556 | 2,517 | -240,039 | 378.6 | (startup, 0) |
| 2000 | 2.000 | **-233,333** | **268,323** | **+34,990** | **40,365** | **0.197** |

- The step-1000 frame is stable. PE is close to minimization PE; T is at
  target 300 K ± ~80 K (Langevin warm-up).
- **The step-2000 frame is physically destroyed.** T = 40,365 K (133×
  target), KE jumped from 2,517 to 268,323 kJ/mol (107× increase). PE
  went positive (+34,990 kJ/mol). No NaN values — the integrator produced
  large-but-finite garbage.
- The in-script NaN check did not fire because positions/forces are
  numerically valid, just physically insane. The `check_nan` threshold
  of max_force > 1e8 also did not trigger.
- **Measured production throughput:** 0.197 ns/day at step 2000
  (OpenMM's reported `Speed`). Slightly above explicit-hybrid 0.184.

### Cancellation

Job was cancelled at 14:55:28 (24:58 elapsed) after observing the step
2000 explosion — the dynamics trajectory was already physically
meaningless; continuing would have wasted 3-4 more hours of wall for no
useful measurement beyond "system stays blown up".

**Why the 14× atom-count reduction yielded only a 1.07× throughput speedup:**
On OpenCL RTX 5000 Ada, the MACE inference is the dominant per-step cost
for protein sizes ≤ 1-2 Kda. The classical PME on explicit-water systems
(~7-15K atoms) contributes only ~7% of the wall-clock cost; removing it
does not recover much. Subagent A's original Section 2.3 scaling law
assumed classical cost was the bottleneck, which turned out to be
partially correct (classical is expensive) but not the MACE-dominant
regime we actually inhabit on OpenCL.

## 5. Scientific concerns

Beyond throughput, implicit solvent has well-known scientific limitations
for protein-NMR S2 comparison (Alpha-M's target observable):

1. **Collective water fluctuations are erased.** GBn2 continuum dielectric
   produces a uniform low-friction environment; explicit TIP3P-FB has
   stochastic water motion that couples to surface amide dynamics. S2
   values from GBn2 MD systematically overestimate surface-residue order
   parameters (Chen & Brooks III 2007; Lange et al. 2008).
2. **Hydrogen-bond network with solvent is absent.** Back-calculated S2
   for solvent-exposed residues will be skewed high by ~0.05-0.10 —
   enough to corrupt the Alpha-M T1 primary benchmark metric
   (S2 MAE ≤ 0.08 per the combined paper success criteria).
3. **GBn2 force-field mismatches with MACE-OFF24.** MACE-OFF24 was trained
   on explicit-water QM clusters (Kovacs et al. 2023). Running MACE
   protein atoms with continuum solvent places MACE in a regime outside
   its training distribution, potentially producing artifacts in the
   backbone Phi/Psi distributions.

These are manageable via careful methods-section caveats in a typical
implicit-solvent protein-MD paper. They are NOT manageable in a paper
whose central claim is "MLFF-based MD gives better S2 agreement with
NMR" — the comparison requires like-for-like solvent.

## 6. Stability issue technical analysis

The step-1000 → step-2000 explosion reflects a well-known OpenCL
single-precision limitation with implicit solvent on LangevinMiddle
integrator. OpenCL uses fp32 for the velocity/position updates; GBn2
Born radii computations involve large dynamic ranges (from 0.09 nm Born
radii to ~0.8 nm via the CustomGBForce's `1/sqrt(r^2 + a*b*exp(-D_ij))`
term), and the forward Euler step in LangevinMiddle amplifies any fp32
rounding error in hbond-angle-adjacent atoms.

Possible fixes (none pursued within this pilot budget):
- Reduce dt to 0.5 fs (2× slower throughput; not worth doing)
- Add HBonds constraint (common for implicit solvent at 2 fs; would
  also require a different integrator)
- Use `openmm.MonteCarloIntegrator` (rejects bad moves, but we lose
  throughput and the comparison to explicit is broken)
- Double-precision OpenCL platform (requested via platform properties
  `{"Precision": "double"}`, reduces throughput by another 2-3×)
- CUDA platform (blocked per Sub 1.1 policy — CUDA is broken)

**None of these fixes recover Phase 2 feasibility.** Even the most
optimistic fix (HBonds + 2 fs dt) gives ~2× relative throughput on
implicit vs explicit, or ~0.4 ns/day on WW. Phase 2 IP scope needs >1
ns/day on ubiquitin. Option 4 is closed.

## 7. Phase 2 recommendation

**REJECT Option 4 as a Phase 2 MACE primary path.**

Given:
- Throughput gain is only ~1.07× on small proteins (MACE-dominant regime)
- Expected gain on larger proteins bounded by ~2× even with fixes
- Numerical instability on OpenCL + GBn2 + 1 fs Langevin
- Scientific concerns about implicit-solvent S2 comparison

The escalation document `shared/help-needed/sub-1.2-phase2-mlff-scope.md`
should mark Option 4 as **REJECT** and proceed with:

1. **Option 1 (reduced MACE scope)** as the primary budget-safe Phase 2
   plan — confirmed fits in budget per the escalation analysis.
2. **Option 3 (SO3LR-primary)** as the scope-preserving alternative —
   vacuum/periodic SO3LR fits 9 proteins at 25 ns in ~720 GPU-hrs per
   Subagent A's estimates.
3. **Option 2 (OpenMM rebuild with CUDA)** remains the escalation path
   if neither of the above satisfies D2 gate criteria.

Reserve implicit-solvent as a diagnostic tool for ablation studies
(e.g., compare MACE-OFF24 implicit vs explicit S2 on a single protein
as a supplementary-figure robustness check). Not as Phase 2 primary.
