## Env Remediation 2026-04-19

### Root cause

`PYTHONNOUSERSITE=1` in the v1 `so3lr_pilot.sbatch`. env-so3lr's transitive
dep tree (flax → so3lr → mlff, plus pandas/rich/pyyaml/dateutil) is NOT
self-contained; it silently depends on user-site for ~4+ common packages.

### Fix method

Non-destructive: env unchanged, sbatch fixed. The change is 2 lines in
`output/scripts/so3lr_pilot.sbatch`:
- `export PYTHONNOUSERSITE=1` → `export PYTHONNOUSERSITE=0`
- PYTHONPATH now includes `/home/rag88/.local/lib/python3.12/site-packages`
  AFTER the env site-packages.

Verification before resubmit:
```
$ /home/rag88/.conda/envs/env-so3lr/bin/python -c "from so3lr import So3lrCalculator; print('OK')"
OK - So3lrCalculator imported
$ ... python -c "import numpy; print(numpy.__file__)"
numpy 2.4.4 /home/rag88/.conda/envs/env-so3lr/lib/python3.12/site-packages/numpy/__init__.py
  (NOT user-site — env-first precedence preserved)
```

### Why this is NOT an MACE-pattern regression

env-mace has the OPPOSITE contract: it MUST have `PYTHONNOUSERSITE=1` because
user-site has numpy 2.x ABI-incompatible with env-mace's bundled numpy.
env-so3lr ships numpy 2.4.4 (env-first wins), and its transitive dep graph
relies on user-site for common Python tooling. Do not apply one env's
pattern to the other.

### Downstream implications

- **All future env-so3lr SLURM scripts must use `PYTHONNOUSERSITE=0` +
  user-site on PYTHONPATH.** Sub 1.4 production MLFF runs must inherit this
  pattern. Do NOT treat env-so3lr like env-mace.
- **Env-mace SLURM scripts keep `PYTHONNOUSERSITE=1`.** That pattern remains
  correct for the MACE path.
- **Env hygiene lesson:** env-so3lr's Sub 0.1 build is incomplete (common
  deps not pinned). Rebuilding a fully self-contained env-so3lr is an
  option but is out of scope for Sub 1.2; the sbatch fix is sufficient.

## §2 Empirical Stability Results (2026-04-21)

performed on login node (CPU only) using env-mace (numpy, h5py). Two
independent stability checks applied: (A) thermodynamic log parsing and
(B) structural integrity from HDF5 position data.

### §2.1 Thermodynamic Log Analysis

Parsed `stageA.log` for each protein. Columns: Step, E (total = KE+PE), KE,
PE, H (NHC extended Hamiltonian), T (K), time/step (s). Logged every 1000
steps (0.5 ps). Temperature and energy drift computed after discarding the
first 50 ps as equilibration.

**NaN details:**
- WW: NaN onset at step 1,408,000 (0.704 ns). Last valid frame T=311.8 K, E normal.
  SO3LR continued integrating through NaN to step 10M (exit code 0). NaN-before-explosion:
  structure was intact (Rg stable) at the last valid frame before NaN.
- NTL9: NaN onset at step 8,772,000 (4.386 ns). Last valid frame T=313.9 K, E normal.
  However, structural analysis (below) shows NTL9 was already exploding from ~100 ps.

**NHC Hamiltonian drift:** All 5 proteins show large H drift (11-29 eV/ns). This
is the Nose-Hoover chain extended Hamiltonian, not the physical energy. In vacuum
NVT with NHC (chain=3, Tdamp=50 fs, dt=0.5 fs), H drift of this magnitude is a
known artifact of NHC thermostat accumulation error. It does NOT by itself indicate
instability; the physical temperature remains well-controlled at 300 +/- 13 K in
all cases. H drift is NOT a pass/fail criterion.

### §2.2 Structural Integrity Analysis (HDF5 Positions)

**Critical finding: thermodynamic log metrics are INSUFFICIENT for stability
assessment of SO3LR vacuum simulations.** HDF5 position data reveals structural
explosions not visible in the energy/temperature log.

Computed from `stageA.hdf5` (shape: n_buffers x 50 x N_atoms x 3, float32).
Metrics: radius of gyration (Rg) time series, center-of-mass (COM) displacement,
heavy-atom RMSD vs frame 0 (Kabsch superposition).

| Protein | Rg(t=0) (A) | Rg(final) (A) | Rg ratio | COM disp (A) | RMSD final (A) | Structural verdict |
|---------|:-----------:|:-------------:|:--------:|:------------:|:--------------:|:------------------:|
| WW      |  9.18       |  8.98         | 0.978    |  0.20        |  3.28          | INTACT (0.70 ns)   |
| GB3     | 10.61       | 986.46        | 93.0     | 60.68        | 1386.75        | EXPLODED at 0.1 ns |
| GB1     | 10.62       | 10.24         | 0.964    |  0.12        |  3.02          | INTACT (5.0 ns)    |
| NTL9    | 10.43       | 948.90        | 90.9     | 66.35        | 1370.10        | EXPLODED at 0.1 ns |
| UBQ     | 11.34       | 10.99         | 0.969    |  0.60        |  2.24          | INTACT (5.0 ns)    |

**GB3 structural explosion:** Despite reporting normal T (300 +/- 13 K) and stable
E throughout the full 5 ns in the log, GB3 exploded structurally at ~100 ps. Rg
grew linearly from 10.6 A to 986 A. Individual atoms flew apart while the logged
thermodynamic averages remained well-behaved. This is a silent failure mode:
SO3LR's log output does not flag structural disintegration. The extended Hamiltonian
H actually shows the largest drift (29.2 eV/ns) of any protein, which may be a
weak indicator.

**NTL9 structural explosion:** Same pattern as GB3. Rg grew linearly from ~100 ps,
reaching ~949 A before NaN at 4.386 ns. The NaN was a delayed consequence of the
structural explosion, not a sudden instability event.

**WW:** Structure was intact at the last valid frame (0.70 ns) before NaN. The
NaN here appears to be a different failure mode than GB3/NTL9 -- possibly a
numerical instability in the SO3LR potential for this small (534-atom) system,
rather than structural explosion.

**Correction to prior assessment:** The table in §1 ("All 5 SO3LR proteins
COMPLETE") reported all 5 as reaching 10M steps with exit code 0. This is true
at the SLURM level -- SO3LR does not abort on NaN or structural explosion; it
continues integrating. The exit-code-0 / steps-completed metric is NOT a
reliable indicator of simulation quality. Future SO3LR runs MUST include
automated structural integrity checks (Rg monitoring) to catch silent failures.

**Recommendation for Sub 1.3/1.4 planning:**

- The GB3 silent-failure finding means ALL SO3LR trajectories (past and future)
  must be validated with structural checks, not just thermodynamic logs.
- Log-based monitoring (T, E) is necessary but not sufficient for SO3LR vacuum.
