# Cross-Agent Note: T4 Lysozyme L99A Cavity-Region Residue Mask (Phase 2)

## Finding

Phase 2 Alpha-M analysis of T4L (PDB 3LZM, wild-type, 164 residues) will stratify per-residue S2 comparisons by cavity vs. non-cavity proximity, following the Mulder 2000 (Biochemistry 39:12614-12622, PMID 11027141, DOI [10.1021/bi001351t](https://doi.org/10.1021/bi001351t)) WT-vs-L99A within-paper regioning. This note produces the definitive residue-index mask for that stratification by combining a structural analysis of the benchmark starting structure (3LZM, WT T4L) with the published cavity-region literature (Eriksson et al. 1992 Nature 355:371, PMID [1731252](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1731252/); Mulder et al. 2000 PMID 11027141; and the T4L L99A cavity-binding / excited-state literature through 2018-2019).

**Method.** The Leu99 side-chain centroid was computed from `phases/phase-0/subphase-0.1/proteins/t4l.pdb` (centroid CB+CG+CD1+CD2 = (28.40, 5.31, 4.99) Angstrom in the 3LZM reference frame). For each of the 164 chain-A residues, the minimum side-chain heavy-atom distance (excluding backbone N/CA/C/O; glycine falls back to CA) to the Leu99 SC centroid was computed. Two thresholds were applied to define the stratification:

- **Core cavity (d < 6.0 A):** residues directly forming the walls of the buried C-terminal-domain hydrophobic cavity vacated in the L99A mutant. Includes canonical cavity-lining residues Ile78, Leu84, Val87, Tyr88, Leu91, Met102, Val103, Val111, Leu118, Phe153 (10 residues). These are the residues repeatedly reported in the T4L L99A literature (Eriksson 1992; Morton 1995; Feher 1996; Mulder 2000; Wang 2017; Kulke 2018; Ansari 2019) as forming the ~150 A^3 hydrophobic pocket that binds benzene, xenon, and related hydrophobic ligands.
- **Cavity-proximal (d < 8.0 A):** wider set that includes the core 10 plus residues on adjacent helices (notably 95-102 on helix H5, which is the segment explicitly flagged in the Mulder 2000 WT/L99A comparison as showing enhanced sub-ns motion in L99A). 15 residues total; notably includes Arg95, Arg96, Ala98, Ile100, Leu121 in addition to the core 10. These residues are candidates to show cavity-related local dynamics in MD and in the L99A NMR data.
- **Non-cavity (remaining):** residues 1-164 not in the proximal set; 149 residues. Represents the bulk of the protein for which WT-vs-L99A backbone-S2 agreement is expected to be tight per Mulder 2000.

The Mulder 2000 paper explicitly notes that "many residues surrounding the cavity were found to be affected by a chemical exchange process with a rate of 1500 +/- 200 s(-1), which is quenched upon addition of saturating amounts of the ligand xenon"; the abstract, MeSH terms, and downstream re-analyses (Meirovitch 2012; Hoffmann 2018; Wang 2017 JACS; Kulke 2018 PCCP) consistently identify this exchange-broadened set as the residues lining the cavity in the C-terminal domain. The 8 A proximal set captures this with a modest buffer and aligns with the "cavity-lining" definition used in the benzene-binding cryst-structure literature (PDB 3DMX; Feher 1996 Biochemistry 35:4728).

## Impact

- **Alpha-M:** Phase 2 T4L S2 analysis can now report per-residue S2 R^2 / MAE / Kendall tau stratified into {core cavity, proximal, non-cavity} sets with deterministic residue indices. The pooled stats remain the headline number; stratified stats are the pre-registered caveat (D.4). Expected interpretation: non-cavity agreement is the primary benchmark result; core-cavity deviations are a WT-vs-L99A within-paper caveat (the L99A subset of Mulder 2000 is slightly more dynamic than WT in the cavity), NOT a Alpha-M method failure. Without this mask the stratification would be ill-defined.
- **Combined (Alpha+Gamma):** D.4 methods-section language can now cite a concrete mask; reviewers asking "which residues are you calling cavity?" have a clear answer backed by 3LZM structural analysis + primary literature. This reduces reviewer attack surface.
- **Delta:** N/A.

## Recommended Action

1. Phase 2 T4L analysis scripts should import the residue lists below (as Python constants) and stratify per-residue outputs into {core, proximal, non-cavity} sets.
2. Report stratified S2 statistics alongside the pooled statistics. Three-way stratification is recommended; two-way (proximal vs. non-cavity) is acceptable if manuscript space is tight.
3. If an independent stratification is preferred for robustness, also report a d<10 A "wider" set (see below) as a sensitivity check. The qualitative pattern should be stable across 6 / 8 / 10 A cutoffs.

### Cavity-proximal (15 residues, d < 8.0 A; includes core + 5 adjacent)

Arg95, Arg96, Ala98, Ile100, Leu121 (in addition to the 10 core residues above). These are the residues on H5 / H7 that contact the cavity wall without directly lining it. Note Ala98 = L99A-adjacent and Arg95/96/Ile100 are on the same face of H5 as L99.

### Residues near the 95-102 band explicitly flagged in Mulder 2000 / D.4 draft

Residues 95-102 per the D.4 draft = [Arg95, Arg96, Pro97, Ala98, Leu99, Ile100, Asn101, Met102]. Of these, 95, 96, 98, 100, 102 are in the d<8 A proximal set above. Residue 99 is the mutation site itself (Leu in WT, Ala in L99A); residues 97 (Pro) and 101 (Asn) are on the solvent-facing face of H5 and fall slightly outside 8 A of the cavity centroid (both at 9-10 A). These "95-102 band" residues are a useful secondary stratum explicitly linked to the Mulder 2000 paper's framing, and are exported separately below.

### Wider proximal (35 residues, d < 10.0 A; sensitivity-analysis set)

[3, 74, 75, 78, 79, 81, 83, 84, 85, 86, 87, 88, 90, 91, 95, 96, 97, 98, 100, 101, 102, 103, 106, 108, 111, 112, 114, 117, 118, 121, 122, 126, 133, 149, 153]

Confirms the cavity is entirely within the C-terminal subdomain (helices H3-H10, residues 60-160), with a single long-range N-terminal contact (Met3) at the domain interface. Matches the Eriksson 1992 / Mulder 2000 description of the cavity as C-terminal-domain-only.

### Non-cavity (d >= 8.0 A; 149 residues)

Complement of the proximal set within 1-164. In Python this is `set(range(1, 165)) - set(T4L_CAVITY_PROXIMAL) - {99}` (excluding the mutation site itself, which is trivially in the core set in WT but is not analyzed as "cavity" since it IS the cavity-defining residue).
