# Cross-Agent Note: Pin1 WW Residue Extraction from 2F21

## Summary

The Alpha-M manifest declares Pin1 WW domain extraction from PDB 2F21
(residues 6-39, 34 residues). The audit (2026-04-17) raised a YELLOW flag
because 2F21 is the **Pin1 "Fip" mutant** — a loop-1 engineered variant of
human Pin1 — while the S2 reference (Seewald et al. 2007 Nat Struct Mol Biol
14:325-331) was measured on the WT Pin1-WW. The purpose of this note is to
establish: (a) which residues of 2F21 correspond to the WT WW domain, (b)
whether the Fip loop-1 mutations fall within the extraction range 6-39, and
(c) the methods-section caveat needed for the combined paper.

## 2F21 Structural Context

- 2F21 is the **full-length human Pin1 (162 deposited residues, 153 modeled)**
  as an X-ray crystal structure at 1.50 Å. Chain A.
- The Fip mutant was engineered by Jäger, Zhang, Bieschke, Nguyen, Dendle,
  Bowman, Noel, Gruebele, Kelly (2006 PNAS) to investigate WW-domain folding
  kinetics: the WT 6-residue loop 1 of Pin1-WW was replaced with the 5-residue
  G-bulge turn from FBP28 WW. This stabilizes the domain by ~7 kJ/mol and
  speeds folding.
- The WW domain in human Pin1 spans approximately residues 1-39 (N-terminal
  domain). The manifest's `residue_range: "6-39"` extracts the stably-folded
  core of the WW fold (the first 5 residues are disordered in solution).

## Loop-1 Mutation Position

Loop 1 of the WW domain is the first connecting turn between β-strands 1 and 2
(Pin1 residues ~19-24 in standard numbering). In the Fip mutant, WT Pin1 residues
Arg17-Trp22 (loop 1) were replaced with the shorter G-bulge turn sequence. The
exact residue substitutions can be found in Jäger et al. 2006 PNAS.

**Critical finding: The Fip mutations fall WITHIN the extracted range 6-39.**
This means the MD starting structure for Alpha-M will include the Fip loop-1
sequence, not the WT loop-1 sequence. This introduces a small systematic
discrepancy with the Seewald 2007 S2 data, which was measured on the WT
construct.

## Impact Assessment

Expected magnitude: The Fip loop-1 replacement primarily affects loop-1 residues
themselves and their immediate neighbors (±3 residues). WW-domain β-strands 2 and
3, strand-to-strand contacts, and the conserved ligand-binding face are unaffected
structurally (Fip folds to WT-like topology with the same native contacts). S2
values on β-strand residues (the majority of reported Seewald 2007 values) should
be comparable to WT within 0.05 units, based on Jäger et al.'s structural
characterization showing Fip is a near-WT fold.

For the Alpha-M benchmark:
- **Loop-1 residues (approximately 5-7 residues)**: Expect larger apparent
  deviations between simulated (Fip MD) and experimental (WT) S2. Flag as
  known caveat, not methodological failure.
- **β-strand residues (the bulk, ~25 residues)**: Expect negligible mutation
  effect. Direct comparison valid.
- **Turn 2 and C-terminal region (residues 25-39)**: Not affected by Fip
  mutation. Direct comparison valid.

## Recommendation: Methods-Section Caveat

The combined paper (Alpha-M Section on Pin1 WW) MUST include the following
methods caveat:

> "Pin1 WW domain simulations used the 1.50 Å X-ray structure of the human
> Pin1 'Fip' loop-1 mutant (PDB 2F21; Jäger et al. 2006 PNAS 103:10648),
> residues 6-39 extracted. The Fip mutant replaces wild-type loop-1 (Pin1
> residues ~19-24) with a G-bulge turn from FBP28 WW, stabilizing the domain
> by 7 kJ/mol without altering its WW-fold topology. The experimental S2
> reference (Seewald et al. 2007 Nat Struct Mol Biol 14:325) was measured on
> wild-type Pin1-WW, introducing a known reference-state mismatch at loop-1
> residues only. Analyses stratify S2 residues into loop-1 vs. non-loop-1
> subsets; primary quantitative comparisons use the non-loop-1 subset, with
> loop-1 residues reported as a separate category with the caveat explicit."

## Alternative: Swap to a WT Pin1-WW NMR Structure

If the reference-state mismatch proves problematic during Phase 2 analysis,
an alternative MD starting structure is **PDB 1I6C** (solution NMR of the
Pin1 WW domain, WT, 39 residues). This would eliminate the Fip mutation issue
entirely but would introduce the standard NMR-ensemble-to-MD initial-structure
noise (typically <1 Å RMSD differences). Decision to swap should be made in
Phase 2 Alpha-M setup, not earlier.
