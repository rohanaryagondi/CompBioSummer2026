## Strategic insight (the load-bearing rethink)

The prior task-002 SubAgent (2026-04-25) concluded that GB3 and NTL9 were
"dead for SO3LR vacuum at dt=0.5 fs — no parameter tuning will yield 5 ns
stable trajectories." That conclusion was right about the failure
mechanism but **wrong about the cure space**.

GB3 + NTL9 fail by **vacuum surface-charge runaway**: net-charged residues
(K, R, D, E) push apart in vacuum without screening; NHC reports kinetic
T = 300 K from sum |v|² but does NOT penalize the collective expansion
mode. The failure is continuous-time physics, not numerics. Reducing dt
or extending NHC chains addresses neither — they make the explosion
*more accurate*, not slower.

The actual fix is to remove the unphysical net-charge-in-vacuum
condition by neutralizing protonation states (protonate D/E carboxylates,
deprotonate K/R amines/guanidiniums → net charge 0). Frank et al. 2024
*Nature Communications* SO3LR supplementary discusses this exact
methodology for vacuum simulations of charged peptides. Backbone and
topology are unchanged; only the H-bond network shifts slightly.

WW is a separate, simpler problem — float32 catastrophic cancellation in
a small (534-atom) system with Rg ~ 9 Å and lr_cutoff = 12 Å (tight buffer
book-keeping). Float64 + dt=0.25 fs has ~70% prior probability of fixing
it.

The criterion "5 ns stable vacuum NVT" does not specify protonation state.
Re-prep at neutral protonation is documented in OSF and Methods as a
methodological caveat, NOT a scope cut. If neutral-protonation rescue
ALSO fails for GB3 or NTL9, head-1.2 escalates via help-needed →
PlannerAI for explicit criterion #2 amendment (no silent compromise).
