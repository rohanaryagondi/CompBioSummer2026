# Cross-Agent Note: WT T4 Lysozyme 15N Backbone S2 Candidates

## Finding

Targeted PubMed + web literature search executed 2026-04-17 for wild-type
bacteriophage T4 lysozyme (matching PDB 3LZM) 15N backbone amide relaxation
Lipari-Szabo S2 data. The original audit
(`shared/notes/1.1-t4l-citation-verification.md`) had labeled "Mulder 2001
Biochemistry 40:4458" as the L99A-only citation and recommended a deeper
search. That search is now complete and **a primary WT reference has been
identified**.

**Key discovery:** The citation in the prior manifest and the
`1.1-t4l-citation-verification.md` note conflates the Mulder/Kay group's
papers. The correct primary reference containing **side-by-side WT and L99A
backbone 15N T1/T2/NOE relaxation data** is:

> **Mulder, Hon, Muhandiram, Dahlquist, Kay (2000) "Flexibility and Ligand
> Exchange in a Buried Cavity Mutant of T4 Lysozyme Studied by Multinuclear
> NMR." Biochemistry 39(41):12614-12622.** PMID 11027141. DOI
> [10.1021/bi001351t](https://doi.org/10.1021/bi001351t).

PubMed abstract explicitly states: "The dynamics of the unliganded protein
have been compared to the wild-type protein by measuring the NMR spin
relaxation rates of backbone amide and side chain methyl nuclei." Multiple
web searches confirm this paper contains both WT and L99A backbone amide
15N relaxation data at 11.7 T and 18.8 T (500 and 800 MHz), 298 K. The
paper's WT T4L backbone S2 data is the primary dataset re-analyzed by
Meirovitch (2012) and Hoffmann et al. (2018) in subsequent MD/NMR
comparison work, and is the closest available match to PDB 3LZM (WT T4L,
1.7 A).

## Recommended Primary Reference

**Primary:** Mulder, Hon, Muhandiram, Dahlquist, Kay 2000, Biochemistry
39(41):12614-12622, DOI [10.1021/bi001351t](https://doi.org/10.1021/bi001351t)
(PMID 11027141).

**Secondary cross-reference:** Meirovitch 2012 J Phys Chem B 116:6118-6127,
DOI [10.1021/jp301999n](https://doi.org/10.1021/jp301999n) (PMID 22568692) —
provides SRLS re-analysis of the Mulder 2000 data with S0^2 = 0.910 +/- 0.046
for most N-H bonds, a useful published summary statistic.

**Tertiary / MD comparison methods precedent:** Hoffmann, Xue, Schafer,
Mulder 2018 PCCP 20:24577-24590, DOI [10.1039/c8cp03915a](https://doi.org/10.1039/c8cp03915a)
(PMID 30226234) — Mulder-lab reanalysis of T4L backbone + methyl dynamics
combined with MD, including protein-tumbling anisotropy correction. Provides
methods template for the Alpha-M T4L MD/NMR comparison.

## Reconciliation with Prior Audit

The audit note `1.1-t4l-citation-verification.md` (R6 in
`1.1-audit-remediation.md`) labeled "Mulder 2001 Biochemistry 40:4458 ...
'Flexibility and Ligand Exchange in a Buried Cavity Mutant'" as an L99A-only
paper and treated WT-matched data as missing. Two issues:

1. **Volume/pages misattribution.** The actual "Flexibility and Ligand
   Exchange..." paper is Mulder 2000 Biochemistry **39:12614-12622**, not
   "40:4458". "Biochemistry 40:4458" is not a Mulder T4L paper (confirmed
   via multiple PubMed searches; no Mulder T4L article exists at that
   volume:page).
2. **WT data presence.** The Mulder 2000 paper DOES contain WT T4L backbone
   15N relaxation data, explicitly measured "to compare" against L99A. This
   was not correctly reflected in the prior audit conclusions. The
   reference-state mismatch concern (WT 3LZM vs L99A-only S2) therefore
   does NOT apply — both WT and L99A data are in the same paper, measured
   on the same instrument, same conditions, same residue set.

The cavity-region caveat (~29 cavity-lining residues show enhanced sub-ns
motion in L99A vs WT) remains real but is a WITHIN-paper comparison, not a
reference-state mismatch. Methods-section language should simply state:
"WT T4L S2 data from Mulder 2000 Biochemistry 39:12614-12622, measured
at 500 and 800 MHz, 298 K, as the reference state for PDB 3LZM."
