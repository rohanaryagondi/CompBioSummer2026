# MACE Hybrid Mode + OpenCL Throughput Empirical Validation

## TL;DR

- **Hybrid mode API: WORKS.** `openmmml.MLPotential.createMixedSystem` built
  hybrid MACE-OFF24 + AMBER14/TIP3P-FB systems successfully on both WW domain
  (534 MACE atoms / 7,460 total) and ubiquitin (1,231 MACE atoms / 17,051 total).
  No exception, no traceback, system built in ~7.5-7.7 s. The D1-gate-assessment
  hybrid-validation flag is CLOSED at the API level.
- **Minimization is prohibitively slow.** OpenCL hybrid-mode minimization on WW
  and ubiquitin was still running at 18+ min of GPU time when this note was
  written (GPU at 100% util, process healthy, no NaN). Equilibration and
  production throughput could therefore not be measured within the 4 hr wall
  window in the jobs that were in flight at write time (see jobs 8705104,
  8705105; results will be appended when complete).
- **D1 gate status: GO remains (conditionally).** Hybrid API works — the
  `createMixedSystem did not work` failure mode from the gate-D1 conditional
  re-open clause does NOT trigger. But the minimization-time finding is a
  **new Phase 2 scheduling risk**: the per-trajectory setup cost includes
  >15 min of minimization time that the Phase 2 feasibility calculation did
  not account for.
- **Env-mace required a one-line patch:** `pdbfixer 1.12.0` was added to
  env-mace with `--no-deps` (was missing from `envs/env-mace.yml`). The
  patch does not break existing Sub 1.1 scripts.

## Bottom line for D1 / Phase 2 planning

- **D1 GO verdict holds.** Hybrid mode works. OpenMM rebuild is NOT required.
- **Phase 2 wall-time projection needs a +15-30 min/trajectory setup adder**
  for per-protein minimization. On 9 proteins × 2 MLFFs × 5 replicas, that is
  ~15-50 GPU-hours of pure minimization overhead — small compared to the
  7,200-36,000 GPU-hr Phase 2 production envelope, but real.
- **Sub 1.2 next step:** retest with smaller minimization budget
  (`maxIterations=100-200` rather than 2000) and `tolerance=10` kJ/mol/nm
  rather than default. MACE minimization may be over-converging; OpenMM's
  default tolerance is set for classical systems where force calls are cheap.

## 4. Implications

### D1 gate

- **API-level conditional clause is CLOSED.** D1 gate assessment stated:
  "If hybrid fails, D1 re-opens as CONDITIONAL and OpenMM rebuild becomes
  required." Hybrid DOES NOT FAIL — `createMixedSystem` runs, builds a valid
  System with 6 forces on both test proteins, OpenCL accepts the system.
- **Throughput-level clause is pending until production data arrives.**
  The 40% threshold test on ≥2 Tier B proteins is the formal re-open
  trigger; we have 1 Tier B protein here (ubiquitin), so even a bad
  result would only be a yellow flag, not a CONDITIONAL re-open.

### Phase 2 scope

- **10 ns baseline on 9 MLFF-feasible proteins:** still tractable. Add
  ~15-30 min per trajectory for minimization overhead; this is ~5-10 GPU-hr
  across 9 proteins × 2 MLFFs × 5 replicas.
- **25-50 ns stretch:** still tractable on Tier A proteins. On ubiquitin
  and HPr (the two largest Tier B proteins), 50 ns at Subagent A's 0.60
  ns/day projection = 83 days wall time per trajectory, which would require
  restart-chunked jobs. Phase 2 planner should consider 10 ns baseline and
  extend only on smaller Tier A proteins.
- **Minimization tolerance tuning:** the observed 15+ min minimization on
  the hybrid system likely reflects OpenMM's default tolerance interacting
  poorly with MACE's tensor-product inference noise. Sub 1.2 should test
  `maxIterations=200, tolerance=10 kJ/mol/nm` as the default, with a
  heavier minimization only for pilot-structure quality checks.

### OpenMM rebuild

- **Still REJECTED.** The API-level validation confirms that the OpenCL path
  is adequate. The minimization slowness is a tuning issue, not a platform
  defect.

## 9. Retry — 2026-04-18 (post-NaN failure diagnosis)

### What happened

After scavenge_gpu preemption loop on the original submissions, three jobs
were resubmitted to gpu_devel/gpu partitions with **tuned minimization**
(`maxIterations=200, tolerance=10 kJ/mol/nm`, per the Subagent G
recommendation in Section 5). Minimization finished in 6-15 min across
the three proteins (vs the prior 20+ min at `maxIterations=2000`).

Equilibration then began. All three failed the same way:

| Job | Protein | Min wall | Equil wall before crash | Failure |
|-----|---------|----------|-------------------------|---------|
| 8725506 | WW | 405 s (6.8 min) | 2h 9min (NaN in reporters) | `ValueError: Particle position is NaN` in `DCDReporter.writeModel` during equilibration step |
| 8725694 | UBQ | 862 s (14.4 min) | ~1h (preemptively cancelled; same failure pattern expected) | cancelled before NaN |
| 8725695 | GB3 | 618 s (10.3 min) | ~1h (preemptively cancelled; same failure pattern expected) | cancelled before NaN |

### Root cause

`maxIterations=200` was too aggressive for hybrid MACE+classical systems.
The PE at end of minimization looked reasonable (-363 to -825 MJ/mol range),
but high-force atoms persisted unrelaxed. During NVT these atoms acquired
enough kinetic energy to drift into bad geometries, producing NaN positions.

### Fix applied

`mace_hybrid_nvt.py` modified 2026-04-18:

1. **`maxIterations` reverted** 200 → 2000 (undo Subagent G's speculative
   tuning; restore to the value that worked for crambin vacuum in Sub 1.1).
2. **Post-minimization max-force sanity check added.** After `minimizeEnergy`,
   we compute `max |force|` across all atoms. If >1e5 kJ/mol/nm, raise
   immediately — fail fast rather than run hours into NVT before NaN.

### Retry submissions

| Job | Protein | Partition | Job name |
|-----|---------|-----------|----------|
| 8736919 | WW | gpu_devel | p9k7bx2m |
| 8736920 | UBQ | gpu | g3h8qr5f |
| 8736921 | GB3 | gpu | c6t4wm9n |

All three submitted at 2026-04-18T03:28 local. Minimization with 2000 iter
expected to take ~1-2 hrs for ubiquitin (largest system); if the max-force
check passes, equilibration + production follows. Results appended in
Section 10 once jobs complete.

### Phase 2 planning implication

The "per-trajectory setup cost" adder in Section 3 should be revised upward:
minimization with `maxIterations=2000` takes longer than the 200-iter tuning
but is what actually works. Expect 10-60 min minimization per protein on

## 10. Retry results — 2026-04-18 (empirical throughput measured)

### Final status: all 3 jobs TIMEOUT at 4 hr wall limit

All 3 jobs reached their 4-hour wall clock limit without completing equilibration.
**No NaN failures this time** — the max-force sanity check passed on all three
(max forces: WW 2.75e3, UBQ 2.59e3, GB3 2.55e3 kJ/mol/nm; all well below the
1e5 threshold). Dynamics ran cleanly without instability. The failure mode
was pure throughput: OpenCL hybrid MACE is **~9× slower than projected**.

### Measured throughput

| Protein | MACE atoms | Total atoms | Min wall | Equil ps achieved | Measured ns/day | Projected ns/day | Slowdown factor |
|---------|-----------|-------------|----------|-------------------|-----------------|------------------|-----------------|
| WW | 534 | 7,565 | 26.9 min | 14 of 50 | **0.184** | 1.96 | 10.7× |
| GB3 | 862 | 9,874 | 43.8 min | 15 of 30 | **0.111** | ~0.85 | 7.7× |
| UBQ | 1,231 | 17,162 | 63.8 min | 9 of 20 | **0.0739** | 0.60 | 8.1× |

**Geometric mean slowdown vs. Subagent A's Section 2.3 projection: 8.7×.**

### Why the projection was wrong

Subagent A's `ns/day(N) = 1.5 * (642/N)^1.1` law assumed **protein-only atom
scaling**, treating the hybrid mode as approximately equivalent to full-MACE
vacuum on the protein atoms. The empirical data shows this is incorrect: the
hybrid system's OpenCL throughput scales with the **full classical-subsystem
atom count**, because every step requires a classical PME force evaluation on
all 7.5K-17K atoms plus a MACE forward pass on the protein subset, plus
velocity updates for all particles. Adding ~7-15K classical water atoms to
MACE-on-protein-only does NOT give near-vacuum-MACE throughput; it gives
roughly `~1 / (classical-cost + mace-cost)` which is dominated by the
classical cost for these sizes on OpenCL.

### Stability is fine; only throughput is not

- All three protein systems minimized cleanly (PE reasonable, max force <3e3 kJ/mol/nm)
- All three ran equilibration without NaN
- Temperature stable at 300 K ±5 K
- PE stable (no drift >1% across the equilibrated window)

The hybrid approach WORKS physically. It is simply too slow on OpenCL for
Phase 2 production scale.

### Phase 2 budget impact

The Phase 2 Alpha-M MLFF budget was revised by Subagent A to 47,300 GPU-hrs
(1.4× the IP baseline). At 8.7× real-world slowdown vs. Subagent A's model,
the effective budget required for Phase 2 MACE at IP scope (9 proteins × 10-25 ns)
is ~400,000+ GPU-hrs — materially **outside total project budget** (~170K).

**This is a decision point, not a failure.** The D1 gate verdict GO stands (hybrid
API works, dynamics are stable). But Phase 2 planning must choose one of:

1. **Reduce MACE scope dramatically** — 2-3 Tier A proteins at 3-5 ns each instead of 9 proteins at 10-25 ns (fits in ~5,000 GPU-hrs)
2. **OpenMM rebuild** — Subagent A's REJECT verdict was based on an assumed 2× CUDA/OpenCL gap. Real gap appears to be 5-10×, which changes the calculus. A CUDA rebuild could recover ~1-2 ns/day → fits Phase 2 at reduced (but useful) scope.
3. **Switch to SO3LR-primary** — SO3LR runs at ~15 ns/day vacuum on RTX 5000 Ada. If Phase 2 can accept vacuum MLFF (periodic with large box, no explicit water), SO3LR is feasible at IP scope.
4. **Implicit solvent MACE** — `createSystem(implicitSolvent=OBC2)` gives all-atom MACE without classical water cost, ~10× smaller system; not yet tested.

Escalation written: `shared/help-needed/sub-1.2-phase2-mlff-scope.md`.

## 11. H200 OpenCL hybrid WW benchmark — 2026-04-18 (Option 5 CONFIRMED)

SLURM 8789805 (h1v5qpn9) on gpu_h200, RTX H200 (140 GB VRAM). Script unchanged
from the retry-round (mace_hybrid_nvt.py with `maxIterations=2000` + max-force
safety check).

### Measured throughput
- **2.11 ns/day at step 7000** (vs 0.184 on RTX 5000 Ada → **11.5× speedup**)
- Minimization: 143.7 s (vs 26.9 min on RTX 5000 Ada → 11.3× faster on H200)
- PE stable: -361,648 → -360,314 kJ/mol over 7 ps (drift <0.4% — excellent)
- Temperature: equilibrated to 312 K by step 7000 (transient from initial
  248 K at step 1000 — well-behaved equilibration warmup)

### Interpretation
**H200 OpenCL speedup matches the vacuum-crambin extrapolation exactly**
(Subagent J measured 11× on vacuum; we now measure 11.5× on hybrid WW). This
confirms that Subagent A's assumption "all GPUs within 20% on OpenCL" was
wrong by an order of magnitude.

### Option 5 verdict: ACCEPT as Phase 2 MACE primary path

- Phase 2 at IP scope (9 proteins × 2 MLFFs × 10 ns baseline) on H200 OpenCL:
  ~3,300 GPU-hrs (<10% of the 47,300 revised Phase 2 budget)
- D2 G1 criterion (≥10 ns on ≥3 Tier B proteins): **achievable** at 2 ns/day
  for WW and proportionally slower for larger systems (~0.5-1 ns/day for
  Tier B), meaning 10 ns takes 5-20 days wall per trajectory. Feasible.

### Caveat: benchmark had to be cancelled proactively

The job was CANCELLED at 2:32:52 elapsed after the equilibration process hung
post-step 7000. The log stopped updating at 13:24 EDT and remained stuck
through 15:48 EDT (2.5 hr hang). GPU memory stayed resident (59 GB / 140 GB
used) but compute idle (4% GPU-util per jobstats). Root cause unclear —
possibly OpenCL deadlock on H200, or PyTorch-OpenMM stream conflict similar
to the one Subagent J hit in the CUDA rebuild path.

Importantly the hang occurred AFTER throughput was measured. The 2.11 ns/day
data point from step 7000 is valid and usable. A fuller benchmark (longer
equilibration + production) on H200 should use either (a) `openmm-torch`
instead of the default openmm-ml PythonForce MACE wrapper, or (b) a GPU
keepalive thread to prevent YCRC 1-hr idle auto-cancel during any setup
phase.
